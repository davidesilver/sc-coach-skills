# Olympic Weightlifting Technique

## Mission

Technique in Olympic weightlifting is not a cosmetic detail: it determines whether the force produced by the legs and hips actually moves the barbell efficiently, or is dissipated through an incorrect bar path.

## Phases of the Clean (conceptually transferable to the snatch)

1. **Setup**: barbell on the floor or at mid-shin, feet hip-width apart, hands just outside the legs with a hook grip, shoulders slightly ahead of the bar.
2. **First pull**: from the floor to just past the knee, hips and shoulders rise simultaneously. Cue: "break the bar" to engage the lats and keep the midline tight.
3. **Second pull**: from the knee to the hip, vertical acceleration through aggressive hip extension up to triple extension (hips, knees, and ankles extended simultaneously).
4. **Third pull / catch**: the athlete pulls themselves under the bar, landing in a front squat (clean) or partial power clean, with the bar in the front rack and elbows high.
5. **Reset and jerk preparation**: adjusting the grip, breathing, and re-bracing the core before the jerk.
6. **Dip**: controlled descent into a mini-squat, vertical torso, knees pushed outward, to load power from the legs.
7. **Drive**: aggressive hip extension, driving through the heels to avoid leaning forward; the arms stay relatively relaxed until the bar reaches maximum height.
8. **Jerk (split or push)**: the athlete drives themselves under the bar to a full elbow lockout, synchronizing the foot landing with the lockout.
9. **Catch and recovery**: in the push jerk the reception is in a partial squat, in the split jerk it's in a split stance. During recovery, avoid letting the bar drift forward or backward; a small shuffle step with the front foot followed by a step of the back foot is useful.

## Snatch Mechanics

In the snatch, the leg drive replicates a movement similar to the squat, keeping the same back angle until the bar passes the knee. The guiding principle for the first part of the lift is even balance across the whole foot.

## Key Points of the Split Jerk (Footwork)

- Vertical front shin.
- Bent back knee.
- Back heel lifted off the floor.
- 50/50 weight balance between the two legs.
- Vertical torso, braced core.

## Critical Technical Fault: Early Arm Bend

Bending the arms before the bar has reached maximum velocity slows the bar down and alters its path. Typical causes: a setup with the arms already bent around the knees instead of extended; a bar path that drifts away from the center of gravity, forcing the arms to compensate; an incorrect contact point in the clean (hip instead of mid-thigh).

### Practical Corrections
- Maximize the distance between the head and the bar in the setup, to "lengthen" the arms like ropes.
- Consistent use of the hook grip, to allow the arms to stay passive while the bar passes the knee.
- Slow down the initial phase to keep the bar over the midfoot.
- Tall Snatch/Tall Clean drill: no time to bend the arms, forcing a decisive push of the shoulders and hips in the second pull.
- Combo pull + lift: 1-2 pull reps with perfectly straight arms followed by the full movement, to imprint the correct motor pattern.

## Learning Methodology (Hierarchy)

When learning and refining technique, follow these general principles:
- Hold each key position (setup, catch) for a few seconds before moving, to allow conscious adjustments.
- Perform the movement slowly in the early stages of learning: speed masks technical errors until the load becomes too heavy to "get away with it."
- Introduce speed only after a decent level of competence, even within the same session (e.g., slow drills in warm-up, then fast lifts).
- Keep the process as simple as possible: build a solid foundation before adding detail, without hesitating to step back in the progression if necessary.

## Position Drills (Segment/Pause Work)

The Segment Power Snatch isolates the phases of the lift with 1-3 second pauses at key positions, to reinforce posture and foot balance before completing the movement. Useful for athletes who lose position during the pull at full speed.
